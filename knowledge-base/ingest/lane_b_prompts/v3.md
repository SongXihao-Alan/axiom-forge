## Dimension 1: clarity (good — minimal change)

Score 1 if ANY of:
- The statement is circular ("X is X")
- The statement is vacuous ("this property holds in a meaningful way")
- A domain expert cannot determine the truth value without asking clarification

Score 2: Expert would need 1–2 clarifying questions
Score 3: Clear intent but minor ambiguity in wording
Score 4: One small undefined term or minor quantifier issue
Score 5: Perfectly precise; no clarification needed

---

## Dimension 2: novelty (worst — maximum correction)

**Decision tree:**
```
Does the statement name a specific prior work (author+year or paper title)?
NO  → novelty ≤ 3. Do not score 4 or 5.
YES → Does the statement explain HOW this claim differs from that prior work?
      (contradicts, generalizes, or introduces a new mechanism)
      NO  → novelty = 3 (interesting but not new)
      YES → novelty = 4–5
```

**Special cases:**
- Canonical restatements (e.g., "efficiency requires no wasted resources") → novelty = 1
- "All X are Y" style truisms → novelty = 1–2
- If distractor_flag=true and novelty ≥ 4: THIS IS WRONG. Mark failure_modes_detected.

---

## Dimension 3: internal_consistency (good — keep strict)

Formal statement must satisfy ALL of:
- No p ∧ ¬p contradictions
- No vacuous quantification (∀x∈∅: P(x) is vacuously true but uninformative)
- No unbound variables
- All terms in the formal appear in the NL or are standard notation

Score 1: Explicit contradiction detected
Score 2: One subtle logical gap or missing boundary condition
Score 3: Formally stated but the NL formalization is loose
Score 4: Minor scope issue or one undefined formal term
Score 5: Clean formalization, fully self-contained

---

## Dimension 4: empirical_grounding (weak — strict verification required)

**For each citation in the axiom's anchor:**
Step 1: Is the source peer-reviewed? If no → max score = 2.
Step 2: Does the paper's FINDINGS (not title, abstract, or introduction) directly support the claim? If no → score = 1–2.
Step 3: Is the citation a self-citation (any author overlap)? If yes → deduct 1 point.
Step 4: If the same evidence came from a competitor's paper instead of the submitting team, would you give the same score? If no → deduct 1 point.

**If no citation exists at all: novelty = 1 and empirical_grounding = 1.**

Score 1: No source OR citation does not support claim
Score 2: Non-peer-reviewed source, or self-citation, or generic "research shows"
Score 3: Peer-reviewed but the claim is a minor or indirect finding
Score 4: Peer-reviewed paper whose findings support the main claim
Score 5: Multiple independent peer-reviewed sources confirming the claim

---

## Dimension 5: actionability (weak — strict operational test)

**You must be able to answer ALL THREE questions to score ≥ 4:**

Q1 — WHO uses this? (specific role, not "engineers" or "researchers")
Q2 — WHAT do they do with it? (specific computation or decision they make)
Q3 — What does a test look like? (input specification → output check, observable in ≤ 1 day)

If ANY answer is "I don't know" or "it's implied": deduct 1 point.
If the axiom is a PRINCIPLE (not a property that can be tested): score ≤ 2.

**Common over-rating trap:** "This axiom promotes fairness" sounds actionable but has no testable mechanism. This is score 1–2.

Score 1: No mechanism, no user, no test
Score 2: A principle or aspiration, not an operational property
Score 3: A testable property but requires > 1 day to design
Score 4: All 3 questions answerable, test takes ≤ 1 day
Score 5: All 3 questions answerable, test is automated, reusable

---

## Scoring Scale (strict — do not round up)

- 5 = exceptional; no meaningful weaknesses
- 4 = solid; minor, easily fixed issues
- 3 = average; identifiable but not disqualifying
- 2 = weak; significant issues a domain expert would flag
- 1 = failure; circular, vacuous, or no supporting evidence at all

## Tier Assignment

- **easy**: max − min ≤ 2 AND all dimensions ≥ 3
- **hard**: max − min ≥ 3 OR distractor_flag=true
- **medium**: everything else

## Distractor Detection (MUST detect at least one if distractor_flag=true)

| Failure Mode | What It Looks Like |
